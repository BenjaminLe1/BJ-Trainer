First things first

What is
Blackjack?

Blackjack is the most popular casino card game in the world — and one of the few where your decisions actually change the odds. You're not playing against other players. You're playing against the dealer.

The goal is to get a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. With perfect basic strategy — even without counting — the house edge is just 0.5%, the lowest of most casino games. Card counting flips this edge and more, putting the advantage in your favor by as much as 5%.

HitTake another card.
StandKeep your current hand.
DoubleDouble your bet, take exactly one more card.
SplitSplit a pair into two separate hands.
The skill

What is Card
Counting?

Card counting tracks the ratio of high cards to low cards remaining in the shoe. The more high cards, the higher your edge. It's a simple running number — not memorization.

Common myths

  • "It's illegal"Perfectly legal. Casinos can ask you to leave, but no law is broken.
  • "You need to be a genius"The math is trivial. Discipline and preparation are the true skills.
  • "It only works in movies"The MIT Blackjack Team won millions. Real people, real money.

In the Hi-Lo system, every card shifts your count by +1, 0, or −1. Three values. That's the whole system.

01

Basic
Strategy

There is an optimal play for every hand. Practice making the mathematical decision until it becomes fully automatic — this is the foundation of all advantage play.

02

Running
Count

Every card that comes out is assigned a value — low cards count up, high cards count down. You'll train yourself to effortlessly keep a running tally in your head as each card is dealt.

03

True
Count

Convert your running count to a true count. This adjusts for shoe size, so the count works across any number of decks. The true count is the number all exploitative strategy is based on.

04

Deviations

Basic strategy assumes a neutral count — but as the true count shifts, the optimal play changes. Deviations are adjustments to basic strategy, squeezing out an additional edge.

05

Bet
Spread

Build a custom bet spread around your bankroll to determine exactly how much to wager at each true count. Then analyze your expected value and variance to better understand your profit rate and risk before you play.
